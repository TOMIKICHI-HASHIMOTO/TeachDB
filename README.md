## Why TeachDB?

Current AI systems primarily accumulate information and retrieve knowledge.

TeachDB explores a different direction.

Instead of simply storing information, TeachDB proposes that AI can continuously grow by accumulating and synthesizing human reasoning through long-term Human–AI dialogue.

The objective is not merely to preserve reasoning, but to enable AI to gradually internalize an individual's thinking patterns and evolve into a personalized cognitive engine.

---

## A Conceptual Architecture for Growing AI

TeachDB is a conceptual research proposal that investigates a new methodology for AI development.

Rather than treating AI as a static knowledge retrieval system, TeachDB proposes an architecture in which continuous dialogue becomes the mechanism for accumulating reasoning, refining cognitive structures, and enabling long-term AI growth.

TeachDB does **not** replace existing technologies such as RAG, Knowledge Graphs, Vector Databases, Memory Systems, or AI Agents.

Instead, it functions as a higher-level orchestration layer that integrates these technologies into a framework for cognitive evolution.

---

## Core Concepts

- Grow AI through accumulated human reasoning.
- Continuously refine cognitive models through Human–AI dialogue.
- Enable AI to internalize an individual's thinking patterns over time.
- Build personalized cognitive engines rather than static knowledge repositories.
- Integrate existing AI technologies such as RAG, Knowledge Graphs, Vector Databases, Memory Systems, and AI Agents.

---

## Example

Imagine an AI that has continuously learned from every novel, essay, interview, and conversation of a single author.

Instead of merely retrieving passages from those works, TeachDB aims to accumulate the author's reasoning patterns, allowing the AI to generate new insights that remain structurally consistent with that person's way of thinking—even for topics they never explicitly discussed.
